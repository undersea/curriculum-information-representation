Bachelor of Accountancy structure

The BAcc degree is designed to allow students to complete the first three years of university study towards admission to the Chartered Accountants College (CA) of the NZICA.

The BAcc degree can also be used to meet the academic entry requirements for the Associate Chartered Accountants College (ACA) and must include at least one of 110.309 Advanced Financial Accounting and 110.329 Advanced Management Accounting.

Typical programme of study

Year One
115.102 Accounting
110.109 Introductory Financial Accounting
115.101 Statistics for Business
115.103 Legal and Social Environment of Business
115.105 Fundamentals of Finance
115.106 Economics
115.107 Management Information Systems
115.108 Organisations and Management

Year Two
110.209 Intermediate Financial Accounting
110.229 Management Accounting
110.249 Accounting Information Systems
110.279 Auditing
110.289 Taxation
125.230 Business Finance
155.203 Law of Business Organisations
155.210 Commercial Law

Year Three
110.303 Integrative Accounting
4 x 300-level Accounting/Elective papers (note 1)
1 x Communication paper (note 2)
1 x Marketing paper (note 3)
1 x 200- 300-level Business Elective (note 4)

Notes:
  1. Chosen from 110.309; 110.329; 110.349 110.379; 110.389; 125.320 or 125.330; including 30 credits (two papers) from 110.309, 110.329 or 110.379. Note: Papers 110.309, 110.329, 110.379 and 110.389 are compulsory NZICA topics for the 2010 relevant year.
  2. Either 219.202 or 219.203.
  3. Either 156.200 or 156.231
  4. Chosen from 200 or 300-level with a prefix selected from 114, 125, 127, 152, 153, 155, 156, 157, 178. (One of 125.320 or 125.330 is compulsory for NZICA 2010 relevant year.)

Planning your programme

All BAcc papers are worth 15 credits each and full-time students usually take papers to the value of 120 credits each year.

The recommended workload per semester is 60 credits (four papers), with 75 credits (five papers) the maximum workload per semester. When choosing your papers, remember to check the timetable to ensure you don’t have any lecture, tutorial or examination clashes. You need to plan to study two 100-level accounting papers in your first year. If clashes appear unavoidable please seek advice from the School of Accountancy Administrator.

Every Bachelor of Accountancy course of study includes the following:

Compulsory papers
Papers in Accounting, Business Law, Economics, Finance, Information Systems, Management, and Statistics are specified, and students choose one of two named papers in Business Communication and one of two named papers in Marketing.

Elective papers
  • accounting papers to the value of at least 60 credits at the 300-level
  • a business paper from a defined range of disciplines at the 200-level or 300-level
New Zealand Institute of Chartered Accountants (NZICA)

The BAcc degree is designed to allow students to complete the first three years of university study towards admission to the Chartered Accountants College (CA) or the Associate Chartered Accountants College of the NZICA.

For the ACA College you must include either 110.309 Advanced Financial Accounting or 110.329 Advanced Management Accounting papers in your study programme.

For the CA College you must include 110.309, 110.329, 110.379, 110.389 and either 125.320 or 125.330 in your study programme. As well as completing the BAcc you must undertake one further year of study (the NZICA requires a minimum of four years full-time equivalent approved tertiary study). Your additional year can comprise a selection of undergraduate or postgraduate papers. NZICA is currently reviewing its entry requirements to the ACA College so please check with the NZICA or the School of Accountancy before planning your course.

CPA Australia Requirements
If you complete your BAcc degree with the required subjects (for details refer to www-accountancy.massey.ac.nz) you will be eligible to join CPA Australia as an associate (ASA), and after completing the CPA program and work experience become a CPA (Certified Practicing Accountant).
